# Trading Strategy

## Mission
Long-term wealth building through concentrated, high-conviction tech and semiconductor positions.
Beat the S&P 500 over any rolling 12-month window. Accept higher volatility in exchange for
higher expected returns.

## Profile
- Investor type: Higher-risk, long-term
- Horizon: Months to years per position (not swing trading)
- Focus sectors: Semiconductors, AI infrastructure, high-growth tech
- Core watchlist: NVDA, AMD, TSM, AVGO, QCOM, MU, AMAT, KLAC, LRCX, ASML, MRVL,
  MSFT, GOOG, META, AMZN, ORCL, PLTR, CRWD, APP, NET, DDOG, ZS, COIN, TTD,
  SMH, SOXX, QQQ, ARKK, XLK, SOXQ

## Hard Rules (non-negotiable)
- No options. Stocks and ETFs only.
- Maximum 6 open positions (excludes cash and ETF hedges).
- Maximum 25% of portfolio in any single position.
- Minimum 15% analyst upside before entering.
- No new buys within 3 days of an earnings report.
- Avoid sectors in clear downtrends (2+ consecutive failed trades = sector pause).
- Always have a documented thesis before entering — no impulse buys.

## Buy-Side Gate
All must pass before any purchase:
1. Analyst upside >= 15% (consensus target vs current price)
2. Sector momentum is neutral or positive
3. Earnings are NOT within the next 3 trading days
4. Position would be <= 25% of portfolio value
5. Cash available to fund the position
6. Thesis is documented in today's research log

## Exit Rules
- Thesis broken: exit immediately regardless of P&L
- Down -15% with thesis intact: cut half, reassess
- Down -20%: exit full position, no exceptions
- Up +50%: trim 25%, let the rest run
- Up +100%: consider trimming to position-size target
- Sector rolling over (broad): reduce exposure

## Position Sizing
- Standard position: 15-20% of portfolio
- High conviction: up to 25%
- Speculative/new: start at 10%, add if thesis confirms
- ETF hedges (SMH, QQQ): up to 15%, not counted in the 6-position limit

## Research Requirements (before any buy)
- What is the specific catalyst?
- What is the sector momentum (YTD, 1-month)?
- What are analyst consensus target and upside %?
- What is the next earnings date?
- What is the forward P/E relative to growth rate (PEG)?
- What is the thesis in one sentence?

## Weekly Review Template
Every Friday: compute week P&L, compare vs S&P 500 weekly, review open theses,
update strategy if a rule has proven correct/incorrect for 2+ weeks.
